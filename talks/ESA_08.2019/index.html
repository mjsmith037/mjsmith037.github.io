We collected data from twelve individual host plants: six of which were grown under what we'll call control conditions and six which have undergone 7 years of persistent nutrient supplementation in the form of N, P, and K as part of the nutnet experiment at the Cedar Creek Ecosystem reserve
for each of these twelve plants we clipped a leaf and from an approx 1cmx1cm section, we extracted twenty isolates: ten bacterial and ten fungal
each of these isolates was then grown on a biolog plate, which, for those who aren't familiar, is a 96 ...
So, we get a measure of growth on each of these nutrients, and this allows us to infer a resource niche ...
but the real magic happens when we repeat this for a second isolate, in which case we can look at how much these two isolates eat the same things ...
we can quantify this to get a measure of niche overlap or resource competition, which we can do in an asymetric manner -- getting the effect of A on B and ...
Finally, we can do this for all possible pairs of isolates and put them together to form a network of interactions for the whole community

Alright, that was kindof a whilrlwind of results, but what does it all mean? Over the final few slides, I will breifly sythesize some of these results and try to tie it all back to the biological implications. In brief, I will touch on the complementarity of the binary and weighted network approaches, and...

Comparing Kingdom

  • Degree distributions differ significantly between interaction types
    • in particular, fungi tend to have a larger impact on bacteria than vice versa (especially for control plants)
  • Isolates cluster by kingdom
    • especially in weighted networks (though more noticeable in binary!)
  • Bacteria and fungi differ in their local structure only in control plants

Comparing Treatment

  • Degree distributions also differ significantly beween treatments

But...

  • There are few / weak differences in global network structure between treatments
  • (Binary) networks show many significant deviations from their random expectations
    • but these differences tended to be small

Conclusions

  • Nutrient ammendments level the playing field
    • Fungi tend to have wider niches and greater overlap on bacterial isolates
    • But these differences are attenuated in NPK plants
  • Despite pairwise differences, differences not visible in larger network structure
  • These small resource competition networks seem to be dominated by pairwise effects
    • No evidence of higher-order interactionsli>

Dichotomization throws away information but also accentuates some differences
